About

What is Privacy Stick?

Privacy Stick is a plug and play USB Flash Drive with a configured operating system that gives users the ability to encrypt their online data and leave behind no trace of ever being on the computer. The operating system is set up for user functionality and based on The Amnesic Incognito Live System which was originally created by the U.S. Navy to conceal government communications. There are three user profiles to choose from for different use case scenarios, two of which have the ability to save files within internal storage while still providing the security of wiping session data at shutdown.

Sessions using the Privacy Stick are held entirely in the Random Access Memory (RAM). Random Access Memory is a temporary form of storage that uses binary patterns of electrical charges called bits to store data your computer is actively using and access it quickly. Since it relies on electricity to store information this means that when power is lost to the memory modules, the entire session is forgotten.

Added applications that play into user security include  Thunderbird  email (with build in end to end encryption),  KeepPassXC  (create and store secure passwords), and  The Onion Router  browser with  uBlock  (ad-blocker). Internet connections are forced to go through onion routing completely encryptying your browsing identity.

What is Onion Routing and how does it work?

If you are connecting to the internet, accessing websites, sending emails, etc. "packets" are being sent out with data that includes your location, your IP address, and what it is on the internet you're trying to access. When using an onion network these packets are wrapped with layers of encryption and passed through a series of nodes, each of which pull back a layer only revealing where to send the information next. When the final layer is decrypted, the exit node can then read the destination trying to be reached online and send you forward.

The sender of the packet remains anonymous as each node in the series knows only the location it recieved from, and the location it is sending to. The process is then reversed as data is being sent back from the destination.

 

 

Who is interested in accessing your data?

Cyber Criminals

A hacker with malicious intent can use many tactics to attack or exploit your data. The best form of protection against cyber criminals is being self aware of what you are clicking on online, but even your IP address being accesssed can give attackers the information needed to possibly take over your device, information, and passwords.

An IP address is a digital fingerprint which is left behind in most of the activity you do online. A hacker getting your address could be as simple as tricking you to click on a link to a legitimate website, or using easily downloadable tools and connecting to the same server as you (chatrooms, online games, etc.). The obtained IP can then be used to view your phsyical location, attack your network, access your device through a vulnerable port, or impersonate your device online and frame you for criminal activity.

Data Brokers

Do you notice that when you search for a product online, over the next while you will often recieve advertisements for the item you searched for? Companies use cookies, tracking URLs, tracking pixels, and many other methods to collect information on your online use. This personal data can be packaged and sold to businesses, advertisers, or even given to the government without letting you know. If you spend time browsing the internet or using social media it is highly likely a profile of your data exists.

Intelligence Agencies

Government and law enforcement use technology and systems that track, collect, and analyse the data of citizens. Mass surveillance is intended to counter-act crime, terrorism, and protect national security; in recent years former NSA contractor Edward Snowden revealed the extent to which global surveillance peers into technological lives and infringes on our intellectual freedom. This has lead to criticization over violating the privacy and rights of law abiding citizens around the world, and creating a distrust towards global power.

Why Privacy Stick?

The benefit of using Privacy Stick is the plug and play, hassle free ease of use for online consumers of any type. Users are set apart from the stigma of using encryption for illegal activity with a clear motive of protecting personal privacy, and can feel comfortable knowing that sensitive data is being protected while still being able to use the OS in a clear functional manner. Onion routing featured on Privacy Stick is similar to purchasing a VPN service; the main difference is a VPN connects to a centralized server to transfer data while onion routing relies on a decentralized layer of secured independent nodes.

Protecting online identity and keeping sensitive data seperate from everyday monitored and tracked online use made simple.
